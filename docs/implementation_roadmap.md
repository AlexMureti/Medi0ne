# Medione Pharmacy: 12-Month Global SEO Implementation Roadmap, Ranking Strategy, and Monitoring Blueprint

## Executive Brief and North Star Objectives

Medione Pharmacy is a community-focused health provider in Nanyuki, Kenya, with a growing digital presence anchored in services such as prescriptions, pharmacist consultations, vaccinations, laboratory tests, health checkups, delivery, appointment booking, and skincare/beauty consultations. The public website serves as the digital front door for these services and must communicate trust, accessibility, and clear action in every interaction.[^1] This roadmap translates Medione’s mission and service portfolio into a disciplined, 12‑month program to achieve top‑tier global pharmacy website ranking for priority segments while winning the local map pack for Nanyuki and Laikipia County. The strategy is designed to be aggressive on outcomes and conservative on tactics—fully compliant with healthcare Your Money or Your Life (YMYL) standards and Google’s Experience–Expertise–Authority–Trust (E‑E‑A‑T) expectations.[^2]

Our North Star over 12 months is threefold:
- Dominate local visibility in Nanyuki for core services (prescription, consultations, vaccinations, laboratory tests, health checks, delivery) through a complete Google Business Profile (GBP), NAP consistency, and review velocity.
- Establish national authority in Kenya for “pharmacy” and “pharmaceutical services” intent with compliant, patient-centered content and authority-building links.
- Earn selected international visibility for high-quality, bilingual (English/Swahili) health explainers and service content, supported by technical excellence and ethical optimization.

We will prioritize outcomes over shortcuts, treating compliance and accuracy as competitive advantages. The operating model is designed to compound: technical readiness accelerates indexation and engagement; content钙化 builds topical authority; local signals drive calls and bookings; and authority links from reputable sources reinforce trust. Reporting will be disciplined, with a single source of truth via Google Search Console (GSC), a KPI dashboard, and monthly/quarterly operating rhythms.

Information gaps that must be closed in the first 30 days include: site-level technical audit results (CMS, hosting, CDN, current CWV); GBP and citation status; baseline analytics (GA4, GSC, call tracking); service scope and regulatory constraints (including telehealth policy and prescribing rules); editorial and medical review resources; confirmed ownership/affiliation for personal branding; target markets and localization requirements; backlink profile baseline; and current content inventory with governance status. These gaps do not prevent early execution, but resolving them will improve velocity and precision.

### Strategic Pillars and Success Definitions

Five pillars anchor the program: (1) a comprehensive keyword architecture aligned to services, conditions, medications, and local intent; (2) healthcare-grade technical SEO and performance; (3) content marketing organized by patient journey with medical accuracy and FAQs; (4) authority-focused link building via trusted directories, local PR, and professional associations; and (5) local SEO excellence in Kenya with a bilingual expansion framework. Success is defined as sustained improvements in visibility, traffic, and conversions, not raw rankings alone. Business outcomes include more booked appointments, prescription inquiries handled efficiently, increased delivery requests, and higher-quality patient interactions via phone and WhatsApp. The program aligns measurement to outcomes to prevent optimization from drifting into superficial metrics.[^2]

### Phase 1 (Months 1–2): Technical Foundation and Baseline

Phase 1 establishes the operating system. The team completes a technical audit (crawlability, indexation, robots/sitemaps), sets up GA4 and GSC, verifies GBP, runs an NAP baseline across directories, and deploys core schema (MedicalOrganization, LocalBusiness, FAQ) to service pages. Performance sprint work targets image optimization, lazy loading, CDN usage, minified assets, and server response improvements, measured via Lighthouse and PageSpeed Insights (PSI). Field and lab data guide CWV improvements, and acceptance criteria are defined per template to avoid regressions.[^7][^8][^9]

### Phase 2 (Months 3–6): Content Engine and Local Dominance

Phase 2 operationalizes the content engine. The editorial calendar initiates weekly production with medical/legal review embedded. Pillar pages for vaccinations, laboratory tests, health checkups, and medication adherence are paired with FAQs and conversion CTAs (call, WhatsApp, book). Local landing pages for Nanyuki and Laikipia are published with embedded maps, consistent NAP, and location references. GBP posts are scheduled weekly; review request flows are launched via SMS/WhatsApp/in-store prompts, and a response protocol ensures timely, empathetic replies.[^10]

### Phase 3 (Months 7–9): Authority Building and Multimedia

Phase 3 scales authority and engagement. Link building focuses on trusted directories, local PR around vaccination drives and screenings, associations, HARO quotes, and thought leadership. Multimedia production adds short explainers with transcripts, VideoObject schema, and accessibility best practices. Reviews and testimonials are integrated, with AggregateRating and Review schema applied once sufficient verified volume exists. This approach is value-first and reputation-safe, avoiding manipulative link practices.[^12][^11][^13]

### Phase 4 (Months 10–12): Internationalization and Optimization

Phase 4 prepares bilingual content with correct hreflang annotations for English (en‑KE) and Swahili (sw‑KE). Accessibility upgrades meet WCAG 2.1 AA; compliance alignment for GDPR/HIPAA (where applicable) is reviewed. The program conducts quarterly audits, refreshes content and FAQs, disavows low-quality links if needed, and refines schema coverage across all templates. International SEO implementation is phased, with localized imagery and examples beyond translation.[^14][^15][^16]
